Technology Knowledge

Below are some of the major components of the Stage 4 (Years 7-8) Technology Mandatory. The knowledge and skills required to teach each subject is detailed for each to inform teaching practice.

Agriculture & Food Technologies

A teacher teaching Agriculture and Food technologies to Stage 4 would need to know the significance of food production in Australia and its relationship. Digital Technologies

In order to teach Digital Technologies as part of the Stage 4 syllabus, a teacher would need to be knowledgeable in information systems. Since technology is an ever-evolving industry, teachers would need to be aware of current issues in the world and the action technology could take amidst it.

Technology Skills

In order to effectively teach the many and varied subjects that make up Technologies, a diverse skillset is required of all TAS teachers. Below are some of the focus areas of Stage 4 (Year 7-8) Technology Mandatory and the skillsets needed in order to teach proficiently.

Digital Technologies teacher skills

A teacher teaching Digital Technologies to Stage 4 would have to be skilled in: programming, innovation, systems operation, brainstorming, hardware identification, algorithm design, and critical thinking.

Materials Technologies teacher skills

A teacher teaching Digital Technologies to Stage 4 would have to be skilled in: critical thinking, operating common workshop power tools, commercial pattern interpretation, sketching skills, braintorming skills, researching skills, identifiying common components and materials.
