My Philosophy of Teaching

I believe it is the role of education to empower students to actively function within society, but also have the adaptability and initiative to recognise when they need to be motivators of change. My philosophy of education has two important tenets which I passionately believe in. The first is promoting life-long learning. I see this as always making learning relevant, while also drawing on and validating the experiences of the students in my class.

I believe it is the role of education For me that supports my view that students need to learn more about how to learn, rather than just the content of what they learn. I want to develop and encourage dispositions, capabilities, and proficiencies for lifelong learning that will allow the students I teach to train and retrain for a future yet to be imagined.

My second tenet is ensuring I maintain a student-centred approach to my teaching. I relate this to Learning through an inquiry approach. This pedagogy or more precisely disposition, encourages both teachers and students to use inquiry to drive their own learning and work through their own questions while promoting greater agency and student voice.
